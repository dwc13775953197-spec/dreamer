## Deep Dive

### 1. OpenAI Erdos Breakthrough: A Milestone in AI Reasoning

In 1946, legendary Hungarian mathematician Paul Erdos posed a deceptively simple question: given n points on a plane, what is the maximum number of pairs that can be exactly one unit apart? This unit distance problem remained unsolved for nearly 80 years.

OpenAI general-purpose reasoning model discovered an entirely new family of constructions that disproved the long-held belief about the upper bound. Mathematician Thomas Bloom verified the result and stated he would recommend acceptance without hesitation if submitted to the Annals of Mathematics.

The significance is threefold: first, this was not AI-assisted proof but autonomous generation of verifiable mathematical reasoning; second, it came from a general model, not a math-specific system; third, it signals that AI will accelerate breakthroughs in biology, physics, engineering, and medicine where long-chain reasoning is essential.
