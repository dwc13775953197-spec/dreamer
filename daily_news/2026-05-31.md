1. Anthropic vs OpenAI: The AI Duopoly Takes Shape

Claude Code, Anthropic's programming assistant, became the key growth driver as enterprises integrated it into core workflows.

This multi-cloud, multi-chip strategy gives Anthropic an infrastructure moat that will be extremely difficult to replicate. Both companies are expected to IPO this fall.

2. Project Glasswing: 10,000 Vulnerabilities in 30 Days - AI Security Enters the Deep End

Anthropic's defensive cybersecurity initiative used Claude Mythos Preview to discover 10,000+ high/critical severity vulnerabilities in critical software within 30 days. A 27-year-old OpenBSD bug and 16-year-old FFmpeg flaw had escaped detection through years of traditional security review. The UK AI Security Institute confirmed Mythos as the first model to pass its 32-step corporate network attack simulation.

This marks the moment AI security moved from theoretical to deeply practical. Two challenges emerge: "AI exploitation" (attackers using AI to find zero-day vulnerabilities) and "AI liability" (who is responsible when AI finds or causes security issues). China responded with the "Smart Agent Specification and Innovation Development Implementation Opinion" and included AI governance in the 2026 legislative plan. The US and allies released "cautious adoption" guidelines for Agentic AI.
