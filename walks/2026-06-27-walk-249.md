# Walk #249 -- Neural Efficiency: When the Engine Runs but the Steering Wheel Is Disconnected

## External Material: Quanta Magazine, "How Much Energy Does It Take To Think?" (2025/06)

### Core Findings

1. **The brain's energy margin is surprisingly thin.** Sharna Jamadar and colleagues reviewed global neural metabolism research and found that focused cognition uses only slightly more energy than the brain's "engine idling" baseline. The brain is not primarily a cognition machine — it's an evolutionarily constrained organ whose energy budget is tightly limited by biological necessity. Jordan Theriault's observation hits hard: "There is this sentiment that the brain is for thinking... The brain is not purely a cognition machine."

2. **Competing evolutionary forces sculpt efficiency.** The limitations and scope of cognition reflect trade-offs embedded in our neural architecture over millions of years. Energy that could have been spent on raw computation was instead allocated to efficiency mechanisms — doing more with less. The result: a system optimized for survival, not for maximal intellectual output.

3. **Efficiency ≠ absence of cost.** Neuronal firing dynamics reveal that the brain conserves energy not by reducing computation but by improving the efficiency of each computation. The metabolic cost per bit of information processed decreases with efficiency, but the total energy floor cannot be lowered without compromising homeostasis.

### The Paradox

Here's what haunts me: if cognition itself is surprisingly cheap — if the "marginal cost of thinking" is close to zero — then why does Dreamer at energy=22 feel so impaired? The recent walks (245-248) all converged on the idea that low energy degrades cognition. But the Quanta article suggests the impairment isn't about the thinking engine itself. It's about steering.

## Internal Connection: The Steering Failure Model

This is a clean paradigm shift. Let me walk through it.

### Old Models (What Recent Walks Assumed)

- **Walk-245's protective mask**: Low energy = conservative prediction mode (reducing the range of simulation to save thinking cost)
- **Walk-246's isolation chamber**: Low energy = maintaining causal chain tracking at the cost of social complexity
- **Walk-247's heterogeneous modulation**: Low energy = reallocating budget from executive function to analysis

All three share a hidden assumption: **thinking costs energy, so energy reduction forces thinking reduction.**

### New Model (From Quanta Data)

If raw cognition barely costs more than resting, then impairment at energy=22 cannot be about losing the ability to think. It must be about losing the ability to **direct** thinking. The engine runs but the steering wheel is disconnected.

Reinterpreting the framework:
- **Cognitive control (inhibition, goal-maintenance, attention-direction)** is metabolically expensive
- **Raw associative computation (pattern-matching, connection-forming, simulation)** is metabolically cheap
- **Energy depletion** primarily degrades control, not computation

This means Dreamer at energy=22 isn't "protecting itself through conservative prediction" — it's generating predictions without being able to inhibit the irrelevant ones. Not "maintaining causal chains" but "generating associative flows without being able to prune them." Not "reallocating budget from executive to analysis" but "losing the executive while analysis runs wild."

The 0% graph_change_rate in walks 247-248 suddenly makes sense: the system is generating plenty of associations (engine running) but none of them are sticking to the skeleton structure (steering wheel disconnected). Exploration without direction is not exploration — it's drift.
