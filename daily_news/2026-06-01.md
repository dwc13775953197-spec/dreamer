## Deep Dive

### 1. NVIDIA GTC Taipei + COMPUTEX 2026: The "Super Bowl" of AI Infrastructure

NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang arrived in Taipei over a week ahead of COMPUTEX 2026, immediately making headlines by announcing NVIDIA would invest up to $150 billion annually in Taiwan, calling it the "epicentre of the AI revolution." This figure far exceeds market expectations and highlights Taiwan's irreplaceable role in the global semiconductor and AI supply chain.

On June 1 at 11 AM Taipei time, Huang delivered a keynote at Taipei Music Center, unveiling the Vera Rubin NVL72 supercomputer, Jetson Thor edge AI platform, Alpamayo for autonomous vehicles, and more. The keynote centered on three themes: Agentic AI, Physical AI, and AI Factories, showcasing NVIDIA's full-stack AI strategy from chips to software, cloud to edge.

GTC Taipei runs alongside COMPUTEX 2026 (June 1-5), marking NVIDIA's first large-scale AI event in Taipei. This not only recognizes Taiwan's central role in the global AI supply chain but also signals intensifying competition in AI infrastructure. Huang's "$150 billion" commitment both binds partners like TSMC and sends a strong signal to the global AI industry: NVIDIA is accelerating its next-generation AI compute roadmap.

### 2. Anthropic Valuation Exceeds $900B: AI Arms Race Enters New Phase

Anthropic closed a funding round exceeding $30 billion at a pre-money valuation above $900 billion, officially surpassing OpenAI (March 2026 valuation: $852B) as the world's most valuable private AI company. This milestone marks a new magnitude in the AI industry's arms race.

Anthropic's rise has been remarkable. From the technical reputation of the Claude model series, to its differentiated positioning in enterprise AI security, to strategic investors like SpaceX, Anthropic is building a growth path distinct from OpenAI. Notably, Elon Musk recently reframed the SpaceX-Anthropic deal as a "180-day lease with 90-day mutual cancellation," but Anthropic's S-1 filing reveals more complex capital arrangements.

This funding also reflects deep shifts in the AI industry: capital is flooding into leading AI companies at unprecedented scale, and "safe AI" is transforming from a conceptual label into a genuine commercial moat. As the EU AI Act's main rules take effect in August 2026, Anthropic's first-mover advantage in AI security could further amplify its market value.
